Design for a positive user experience

  • Keep the UI design simple to reduce frustration
  • Be empathetic, understand how the user thinks
  • Have consistent rules so the user knows what is and isn't possible
  • Have responsive controls
  • Impose enough contraints so the user doesn't mess up, but not enough to restrict freedom

Aim for high usability

  • Design so that the user's goal can be effectively completed
  • Have processes be short and efficient
  • Make it easy to learn how to use your product
  • Make it easy to remember how your product works
  • Increase utility by having any feature a user could need

Be mindful of cultural differences

  • If possible, avoid the use of machine translations
  • Research differences and intricacies between cultures
  • Make sure text is formatted appropriately - left to right or right to left
  • Identify primary market devices for the culture you're developing for
  • Design for gender

Reduce barriers to accessibility

  • Use colors that are easily perceivable
  • Be cognizant of what colors to avoid for color blindness
  • Make sure to not rely on sounds so that your product is operable by deaf people
  • Use readable and appropriately sized fonts for people with low levels of vision
  • Have speech recognition for those with motor impairments

Gather requirements

  • Use questionnaires to get quick impressions on your product
  • Be wary of the shortcomings of questionnaires such as lack of context
  • Requirements should be discovered as fast as possible
  • Interviews can be used to get more detailed responses to your product

Use conceptual models

  • Use a metaphor to something the user already knows
  • Make sure the model is relevant to the task at hand
  • Identify whether your model is easily understood
  • See if your model is easily represented
